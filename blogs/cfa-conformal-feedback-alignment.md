# CFA. Weighting AI Feedback by How Reliable Each Answer Is

*Paper "Conformal Feedback Alignment: Quantifying Answer-Level Reliability for Robust LLM Alignment" (ACL ARR).*

Reinforcement learning from AI feedback replaces costly human labels with preferences generated by a model, but that feedback is noisy. Prior work tries to handle the noise by weighting the preference, that is by asking how reliable the comparison between two answers is. This paper points out a blind spot. A preference can only be as trustworthy as the two answers it compares. If both answers are low quality, the preference carries little information no matter how the comparison is modeled.

**The insight.** Reliability should be measured at the answer level, not only at the preference level. CFA uses conformal prediction to estimate, with distribution-free guarantees, how reliable each individual answer is, then folds that reliability into the alignment loss so weak pairs contribute a weaker learning signal.

![The CFA pipeline](https://tiejin98.github.io/blog_image/cfa-conformal-feedback-alignment/overview.png)

*Conformal prediction scores each answer in both black-box and white-box settings, the pair's set memberships collapse to a single weight, and that weight scales the DPO or PPO preference loss.*

## How it works

1. Generate AI feedback by sampling answers and obtaining pairwise preferences from an AI evaluator.
2. Score each answer with a nonconformity measure, using negative log-likelihood in the white-box case and a frequency and entropy based score in the black-box case.
3. Calibrate conformal sets at two coverage levels on a held-out set, which gives a more reliable set and a looser set.
4. Aggregate the pair's set memberships into one weight in the unit interval, lower when the two answers fall in weaker or mismatched sets.
5. Multiply the DPO log-sigmoid term or the reward-model loss by that weight, then train as usual, so unreliable pairs move the policy less.

## What the experiments show

CFA improves on the base PPO and DPO alignment in all eighteen model, dataset, and algorithm combinations, across Llama2-7B, Llama3.1-8B, and Qwen2.5-7B on Summarize, Pairwise, and WebGPT.

![CFA versus base alignment across models and datasets](https://tiejin98.github.io/blog_image/cfa-conformal-feedback-alignment/results.png)

The strongest single point is Llama2-7B on Summarize with DPO, where CFA scores 67.30 against 65.68 for the base, a gain comparable to what the base alignment itself added over supervised fine-tuning. Win-rate comparisons agree, and CFA also beats preference-level uncertainty methods such as WPO and importance-weighted DPO.

## Takeaway

Noisy AI feedback is partly a data quality problem at the source. Measuring how reliable each answer is with conformal prediction, then weighting the learning signal by it, makes alignment more robust and more data efficient than weighting the preference alone.
